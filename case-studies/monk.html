MONK | BRAND IDENTITY + PACKAGING CONCEPT

Brand Identity / Product Concept
Focus: naming, visual identity, brand direction, packaging design.

Concept

MONK is a concept wellness brand I designed that reimagines how people experience sweetness. The brand is built around the idea that natural sweeteners should feel like a premium lifestyle product, rather than a diet substitute or artificial alternative.

Avoiding clinical or overly functional wellness language, MONK aligns its identity more closely with modern skincare and lifestyle brands, positioning monk fruit as a clean, desirable everyday ritual.

Brand direction

  • Developed the product concept, naming, and brand direction.
  • Explored how visual identity, tone, and materiality can shape trust and emotional connection within wellness.
  • All imagery is conceptual and used for speculative brand exploration.

Skills shown

  • Brand naming and concept development.
  • Visual identity direction and packaging design.
  • Understanding of brand positioning within wellness and lifestyle categories.
  • Ability to translate a product idea into a cohesive brand experience.

Visual exploration

Positioning

MONK is positioned as a lifestyle wellness brand rather than a functional food product. The visual language borrows from skincare aesthetics: frosted glass, embossed typography, warm amber tones, and tactile materials. This approach shifts perception from "sugar alternative" to "everyday ritual."
